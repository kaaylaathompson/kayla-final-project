Golden Pothos

Epipremnum aureum

Caring for a Golden Pothos is relatively simple, making it a popular choice for both beginner and experienced indoor gardeners.

Light- Thrives in indirect or filtered sunlight but can tolerate low light conditions. Avoid direct sunlight, as it can scorch the leaves. Place your plant near a window with a sheer curtain or in a room with bright, indirect light.

Water- Allow the top inch or two of the soil to dry out between waterings. Stick your finger into the soil to check for moisture; if it feels dry, it's time to water. Overwatering can lead to root rot, so it's better to underwater than overwater. Water thoroughly and allow excess water to drain away.

Pot- Use a plastic, ceramic, metal, or terracotta pot with sufficient drainage. If your pothos lives in an area with low light, consider using terracotta, which helps wick away moisture.
