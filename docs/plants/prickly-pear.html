Prickly Pear Cactus

Opuntia

Caring for a prickly pear cactus, also known as Opuntia or paddle cactus, is relatively straightforward.

Light- They thrive in full sun. Place them in a location where they can receive at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight per day, such as a south-facing window. If grown outdoors, ensure they receive adequate sunlight and protect them from excessive shade.

Water- Prickly pear cacti are drought-tolerant and prefer infrequent but deep waterings. Allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings to prevent root rot. During the growing season, water sparingly, approximately once every 2-3 weeks. Reduce watering in the winter when the plant is dormant.

Pot- Choose a pot with generous drainage holes at the bottom. It's a good idea to choose a low, wide pot to provide a good balance, as these plants will sprawl as they grow.
