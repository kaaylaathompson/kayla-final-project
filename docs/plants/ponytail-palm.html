Ponytail Palm

Beaucarnea recurvata

Ponytail palms can thrive and bring a touch of tropical elegance to your indoor or outdoor space.

Light- Prefers bright, indirect light but can tolerate lower light conditions. Place your plant near a window where it can receive plenty of sunlight, but avoid placing it in direct sunlight for extended periods, as this can scorch its leaves. If grown indoors, a south- or west-facing window is ideal.

Water- Ponytail palms have a bulbous trunk that stores water, making them drought-tolerant. Water your plant thoroughly, allowing the soil to dry out partially between waterings. In the winter months or when the plant is dormant, water sparingly to prevent overwatering.

Pot- Ponytail palms like to be a little crowded in their containers, so select a pot that is no more than 2 inches wider all around than the base of the plant. It should also have a drainage hole.
